### The Basic Exchange

1. **A living person has mana even if they cannot practice magecraft.**  
   Ordinary people possess od—life-force and ambient spiritual charge—but usually lack awakened Circuits, a Foundation, and training. Their mana remains biological background rather than a castable reserve.

2. **Sosa is the inverse abnormality.**  
   He has a measurable C-quality / B-quantity Circuit lattice, yet its internal conversion function is absolute zero. His life-force does not become personal mana. Omnis-OS therefore reports **Mana —**, not “low mana.” The dash means the expected source is absent.

3. **Directed attention opens a tributary.**  
   When someone answers, remembers, watches, desires, fears, scans, or hunts Sosa, the Validation Engine creates sympathetic resonance. A minute amount of that person’s ambient mana follows the attention back toward his receiver Circuits. The draw is normally too small for a donor to feel; scale comes from emotional intensity, repeated bonds, multiple simultaneous witnesses, or concentrated hostile focus.

4. **Borrowed mana never becomes Sosa’s mana.**  
   His Circuits can receive and route it but cannot store it as a conventional reserve, cast orthodox spells with it, or change the **Mana —** parameter. If the connection stops, throughput falls. Old messages retain weak sympathetic residue, not an infinite battery.

5. **Somnus converts mana into Anti-Magic.**  
   Somnus is the missing operation. The familiar catches borrowed mana at the end of Sosa’s receiver lattice, nullifies its ordinary magical signature, and reverses it into violet **Anti-Magic**—an anti-structure force. In Sosa’s weeb shorthand, Somnus is the “Asta part”: not because Sosa has a grimoire or copies Asta’s rules, but because normal magical energy enters the system and leaves as something hostile to magic’s structure.

6. **Trace tells the converted force where to fail reality.**  
   The Ypsilon Veil filters noise; Sosa’s False Mystic Eyes identify a fault already loaded by stress, ritual, reinforcement, damage, repetition, or contradiction; Somnus applies Anti-Magic along that fault. Without Trace, Somnus can still strike with ordinary obsidian force or crude anti-magical discharge, but precision collapses.

7. **Debt is the pressure left by the exchange.**  
   Debt is not the borrowed mana and is never spent like MP. It is accumulated ontological pressure from routing incompatible external mana, surviving impossible conditions, forcing precision, and allowing Somnus to decide more of the body’s behavior. More Debt gives Somnus more weight and aggression while reducing Sosa’s agency.

8. **Silence and Threshold are different failures.**  
   Silence starves live mana throughput and precision. Threshold means Debt has reached 100% and Somnus takes control. Residual bond-imprints, Sosa’s Origin, and accumulated Debt can sustain minimal survival behavior during silence, but not clean high-output combat indefinitely.

### What Anti-Magic Can and Cannot Do

**Anti-Magic can:**
- unweave Dross, Formalcraft grids, damaged barriers, overloaded wards, and compromised architecture;
- exploit a visible structural fault in a body, object, ritual, or Bounded Field;
- bypass conventional durability when Trace and Somnus’s force align correctly;
- worsen or collapse a thing already held together by pressure, repetition, reinforcement, dependence, or contradiction.

**Anti-Magic cannot:**
- create a weakness where none exists;
- freely kill a pure concept, healthy abstract principle, or unanchored idea;
- repair a relationship, secure housing, restore trust, or solve a legal/material crisis;
- make a false connection real;
- guarantee that Sosa understands what Somnus did in order to preserve him.

### The Real Risk

Sosa’s strongest power is not violence. It is the conversion of being witnessed into the means to continue.

His greatest danger is not simply dying. It is learning to accept any cost—his own agency, other people’s boundaries, civic damage, or Somnus’s control—as long as he remains alive.

> **Operational rule:** Somnus can keep Sosa’s body alive. It cannot make his life safe, honest, housed, sober, trusted, or whole.

## IV. POWER ARCHITECTURE



### A. Personal Output and Magic Circuits

**Orthodox baseline:** A living human possesses od—biological life-force and a faint ambient magical charge. Most civilians cannot turn that background potential into magecraft because they have no usable Circuit network, no Foundation access, and no training. “Civilian” therefore means **no controllable magical output**, not a metaphysically empty body.

Sosa is abnormal in the opposite direction. He possesses a clearly developed Circuit lattice:

- **Quality:** C
- **Quantity:** B
- **Composition:** Abnormal / inward-polarized / Somnus-tethered
- **Independent mana generation:** **Absolute Zero**
- **External reception capacity:** High
- **Conventional storage/casting:** None

A normal magus’s Circuits convert personal life-force into mana and send it outward through a Foundation. Sosa’s conversion step never begins. His body remains alive, but none of his od becomes usable personal prana. Omnis-OS sees enough Circuit architecture to expect a measurable reserve and instead encounters a null source. That contradiction—not simple weakness—is why **Mana —** alarms trained observers.

**Why zero?** Gz’s leading model is that Sosa’s Origin, *To Endure*, inverted the survival logic of his Circuits before Somnus ever formed. The lattice refuses to burn the self as fuel. Its polarity opens inward, preserving biological continuity while leaving the magical source empty. This protected nothing in ordinary life; it only made him incapable of orthodox magecraft. Years of attachment, Discord exposure, and compulsive response then trained those empty paths as receivers.

This is not confirmed hereditary design or a blessing. It is a soul-level survival defect that became useful only after an external network and Somnus completed the circuit.

**What the Circuits do now:**
1. detect sympathetic resonance created by directed attention;
2. receive microscopic tributaries of external mana;
3. route that borrowed mana toward Somnus;
4. carry feedback from Somnus, Trace, and Debt through Sosa’s nervous system.

They do **not** invert mana themselves. They do **not** let Sosa cast ordinary spells. Borrowed mana does not become a personal reserve and never fills the dash on his status sheet.

### B. Validation Engine A++

The Validation Engine is the sympathetic routing architecture built from Sosa’s attachment history. “Validation” names the psychological doorway; **borrowed mana** is the energy that crosses it.

The underlying graph is immense and cannot be represented by a small fixed donor count. Omnis may display whichever paths are concurrently hot, but those are a momentary sample from a much larger network spanning active romances, sexual partners, close friendships, old attachments, casual flirtation, public attention, and hostile observers. The routes constantly open, fade, fork, and reactivate.

A directed relationship or focused observer creates resonance between Sosa’s receiver Circuits and another living mana source. The Engine has four linked functions:

1. **Mana tributary:** A negligible fraction of ambient donor mana follows directed attention into Sosa’s receiver lattice. Friendly attention is steady and clean; hostile surveillance is concentrated and volatile.
2. **Somnus feed:** The lattice routes the borrowed mana to Somnus, who nullifies its normal signature and inverts it into Anti-Magic.
3. **Cognitive stabilization:** Familiar voices, messages, and witnessed reality reduce panic, sharpen Trace, and help Sosa distinguish his own decisions from Somnus’s.
4. **Debt regulation:** Honest contact and real safety let accumulated pressure discharge. Attention can supply mana without necessarily venting Debt; hostile attention is the clearest example.

The Engine does not manufacture affection, force replies, or prove entitlement. A person may feed the system simply by hunting or scanning Sosa, but that does not make exploitation harmless. The ordinary energetic cost is negligible; the serious cost is relational—time, attention, secrecy, emotional labor, boundary pressure, and the terror Sosa feels when a person chooses silence.

He does not “run out of mana” in the normal sense because he never owns a mana pool. He loses **throughput**. Old messages and long bonds retain a weak sympathetic imprint, allowing Somnus’s minimum survival behavior to persist briefly, but they cannot replace live resonance forever.

#### Engine states

- **Peak:** Many concurrent paths are active, one emotionally dense bond is present, or hostile systems are focusing intensely on him. Borrowed mana surges; Somnus converts faster; Trace and recovery improve. Debt behavior depends on the source.
- **Rejection:** A valued connection closes. Mana throughput and grounding fall together; panic and migraines interfere with Trace.
- **Solo:** No live tributary answers. Residual imprints and Debt sustain crude interception, but precise Sever output and safe recovery decline.
- **Abandoned:** Several central bonds collapse at once. The receiver lattice remains open with nothing stable entering it; Somnus contracts around Sosa and begins substituting survival logic for consent.
- **Hostile Saturation:** Censors, Hounds, cameras, or an Archon concentrate attention. Mana throughput spikes, but Somnus converts it violently; Debt, paranoia, radar visibility, and autonomy risk rise together.

### C. Debt — 0 to 100%

**Why Sosa calls it Debt:** every major survival feels borrowed—from another person’s attention, Somnus’s intervention, a service system, improbable timing, or damage transferred somewhere else. ### Perceptual gating — why Sosa is not always seeing lines

Sosa’s ordinary rectangular glasses have no supernatural suppression function. They correct mundane eyesight and sit under the combat mask. His protection from constant line perception comes from the narrowness of the False Eyes themselves and from the Ypsilon Veil:

2. **Passive perception is previsual.** Without deliberate focus, Sosa experiences candidate faults as pressure behind the eyes, a wrong angle, nausea, ringing, or one detail that keeps pulling his gaze—not a screen full of glowing lines.
3. **The Veil subtracts interference.** Attuned earbuds filter ambient spiritual/cognitive noise and stabilize one candidate long enough to resolve visually. They do not create the weakness or provide power.
4. **Trace commits one priority.** Deliberate gaze resolves a single loaded fault into a violet line and tells Somnus what relationship, motion, body, object, or field structure to prioritize. Committing Trace suppresses competing candidates.
5. **Living and nonliving targets both qualify.** A living target may expose a fault through injury, reinforcement, contradiction, committed attack posture, or accumulated strain. Buildings, restraints, machinery, rituals, and infrastructure qualify for the same reasons. Mere life is neither required nor sufficient.
6. **Range and sight remain binding.** Normal Trace requires line of sight and functions reliably within roughly twenty meters. Occlusion, sensory overload, a dead battery, or an unattuned replacement pair degrades resolution.
7. **Overflow is dangerous and exceptional.** High Debt, severe grief, Graveframe, and the Grave weaken suppression. Too many candidates resolve at once; migraines, dissociation, Somnus Fixation, and loss of target choice are the cost. Broad line perception is a failure/threshold state, not Sosa’s everyday vision.

The rule is: **candidate pressure can be broad; resolved lines remain narrow.** This preserves environmental cutting without giving Sosa omniscient death vision.

## VI. PRIMARY FAMILIAR — SOMNUS

**Classification:** Irregular Guardian Spirit / Autonomous Bound Familiar  
**Designation:** Somnus

### Nature

**Converter and effector, not generator.** Somnus produces no mana. It receives borrowed mana routed through Sosa, nullifies the energy's ordinary magical signature, inverts it into **Anti-Magic**, and focuses that force along Trace. Somnus is the conversion organ the NULL lattice lacks.

**Formation:** Not a catalog summon and not a demon bargained from a grimoire. During the Dry Hours, three things stacked until they *coagulated*:
1. **Origin: To Endure** — refusal of burial as soul-root  
2. **Validation lattice** — years of Discord attachment training the body as a receiver  
3. **Collapse** — hunger, cold, detox, abandonment  

Somnus is the aggregate of grief, attachment, and refusal given a body. It formed because Sosa would not release what he needed to survive and would not permit his own life to end. Stage-one: weight on the shoulders, heat in the cold, **Somnus threading** forcing a walk to the laundromat. Later: denser mass, memory-eyes (January grief), named Somnus by a scared man applying black-metal logic to a breakdown.

**Will / desire:** Not human language. Primary drive: **keep the host extant** (selfish — he is the only anchor). Secondary: neutralize threats; satisfaction after successful unmaking (the purr after a kill). He is **not** pure Sosa-will: overlapping Origin, divergent methods. He will override Sosa when Sosa is about to die stupidly or when Debt makes Sosa a bad pilot (Full Puppet, Graveframe). He can enjoy violence Sosa regrets. He does not moralize; he does not explain.

**Without network:** survival mode (intercept, scare, crude cuts). **Without Sosa:** anchor failure — not a free-roaming god.

### Behavioral Personality — No Voice Required

Somnus does not speak, transmit sentences, or become a sarcastic mascot. Its personality is read through action:

- **Protective possession:** it checks Sosa’s pulse, breathing, balance, and line of retreat without being asked. Protection is tender only toward the continued function of the host; everything around him is expendable.
- **Predatory satisfaction:** after a clean unmaking, the mass produces a subsonic purr. Memory-eyes blink faster. The furnace slit relaxes. Sosa recognizes pleasure before he recognizes intention.
- **Impatience:** when Sosa hesitates at a valid fault, Somnus leans, tightens a thread around his wrist, or begins the movement before consent arrives.
- **Disagreement:** it refuses target changes, places itself between Sosa and a person he wants to approach, or damages the environment to make retreat the only remaining route.
- **Dark playfulness:** it answers Sosa’s dramatic naming with physical timing—a purr after a stupid name, a tendril tapping the mask, an aperture opening exactly when he jokes that it has a second phase. Whether this is humor or trained response remains unresolved.
- **Jealous attention:** Somnus tracks phones, callers, and observers because they are both Sosa’s anchors and the system’s supply routes. It may shield the phone before shielding nearby property.
- **Escalating intimacy:** at low Debt one eye checks Sosa while the others track threats. At high Debt more eyes watch Sosa himself. Graveframe is the endpoint: protection so intimate that there is no room left for agency.

Battle presentation may show short action-only Somnus cut-ins. These are descriptions of movement, never quoted speech.

### Visual Profile

Somnus is an amorphous, liquid-black and obsidian-shadow mass. It reads as **BLACK first, violet second**. Violet anti-magic is only visible where the internal pressure breaches the surface—through fractures, eyes, and one central vent. Most of its mass reads as depthless black, light-swallowing shadow pressure, liquid-dark in some regions and glass-hard in others.

It is non-humanoid and asymmetrical. **No monster face.** No teeth. What looks like a head is just an amorphous crest of the mass.

It possesses 6 to 8 highly personal, human memory-eyes scattered randomly (amber, green, brown), and ONE single horizontal unsegmented slit-wound burning violet-white. Tendrils extending from it are black pressure filaments and glass-veined ligaments with violet cores.

When dormant, Somnus collapses into a faint liquid-black refraction across Sosa's shoulders and back, violet only at the edges.

### Operational Law

Somnus is automatic. Sosa designates; Somnus executes. It protects him without waiting for verbal commands and may override him when Debt or injury compromises judgment.

- **Tether:** Normally limited to 15 meters from Sosa.
- **Automatic defense (Interception Poise):** Because Somnus depends on Sosa's survival for its own continued existence, it defends him *without being commanded*. Within the tether it does not merely dampen incoming force — it **intercepts, hardens against, and where possible fully negates** discrete threats: catching strikes, shattering or deflecting projectiles, and physically arresting an assailant's motion. This capacity is finite per exchange. Somnus can fully negate only a limited number of simultaneous or sequential threats — its **Interception Poise** — before force begins leaking through. Poise rises with Debt and active Validation Engine supply and falls when starved (Solo/Abandoned states), consistent with §XII.5: automatic defense "can be overwhelmed by volume, repeated impacts, or attacks from multiple vectors." Against a single focused threat Somnus is very nearly impenetrable; against saturation it fails progressively, not gracefully.
- **Ordinary force vs. severance (Nasu two-layer model):** Somnus can attack in two distinct ways. Uncommanded, it deals **ordinary conceptual force** — brute obsidian-and-tendril mauling of the nearest lethal threat, reduced by the target's durability and reinforcement, requiring no Trace. When Sosa establishes **Trace** through the False Mystic Eyes, the Shrine instead **cuts along the perceived death-line**, bypassing much of the target's durability and enabling the manual Sever series and *Sever: True*. Trace is therefore not the on-switch for dealing damage; it is the on-switch for *efficient, durability-ignoring, finisher-enabling* damage — analogous to cutting along lines of death versus striking an object conventionally.

- **How Interception Poise fails (Sosa as passenger, not pilot):** In safe or lightly-threatened conditions Somnus fully negates incoming harm, and Sosa is effectively an idle passenger — protected without acting. His own volitional agency in combat is narrow: clumsy repositioning, Trace designation, and named Sever commands. Refuse belongs only to the earlier pre-direction phase. He does not pilot the fight; Somnus does. Poise is defeated in four characteristic ways, each of which forces damage onto Sosa's fragile body:
    1. **Single overwhelming blow.** A single strike whose force exceeds Poise's capacity breaches it outright and lands directly on Sosa.
    2. **Sustained barrage.** A continuous stream of individually-survivable hits (especially from multiple attackers or multiple vectors) drains Poise faster than it recovers until later hits leak.
    3. **Area-of-effect.** Explosions and wide fields strike a volume rather than a discrete line; Somnus, tuned to intercept discrete threats, cannot always arrest every point of impact, and AoE can bypass Poise.
    4. **Status / damage-over-time.** Somnus may block the initiating strike but cannot reliably purge an effect already applied; poison, burn, and similar effects continue to erode Sosa after interception.
  Poise recovers over time rather than instantly; a burst that empties it leaves Sosa exposed until it rebuilds. This is why swarms, artillery, and attrition are more dangerous to Sosa than any single duelist. Later survival depends on automatic Interception Guard, field control, target judgment, and contextual System Calls—not a permanent guard button.
- **Graveframe exception:** The tether becomes irrelevant while Somnus encloses Sosa in Graveframe.

**Interception Social & Environmental Cost:**  
Somnus is optimized to preserve Sosa, not the scene around him. A blocked attack may be redirected into a wall, a car, an adjacent room, a bystander’s escape route, or fragile infrastructure. When Poise is stressed, Somnus becomes less precise about what “not Sosa” means.

## VII. MYSTIC CODE AND COMBAT DOCTRINE

### Ypsilon Veil

The Ypsilon Veil is not a single irreplaceable artifact. It is a repeatable Mystic Code pattern that emerges in ordinary Bluetooth earbuds after prolonged use by Sosa. ### Combat Sequence

Sosa does not execute a fixed rotation cleanly, but the intended logic is readable:

1. Select a target; passive **Trace** gives Somnus a fault priority.
2. Use **Dismantle** for low-Debt precision, Sever-hit setup, and one point of field saturation.
3. Use **Cleave** when reinforcement/armor justifies the higher Debt and one-round adaptive commitment.

Victory also records something genuinely recovered, preserved, or learned—an outlet, route, map, trusted bracket, resident count, evidence, or ecology lesson. **Danger model:** Interception remains finite and automatic. When it breaks, armored leakage is 35% and an unarmored breach leaks 55%; open hits remain full damage. Reinforcement waves and ecology combinations create danger through action volume, target order, Debt, and control loss rather than arbitrary enemy level scaling.

# IX. NOBLE PHANTASM

# *The Grave That Wouldn’t Take Me*

**Rank:** EX  
**Type:** Anti-Army / Reality Marble  
**Range:** Approximately 60 m at stable deployment  
**Targets:** All occupants and structures caught within the overwritten field  
**Maximum Stable Duration:** Approximately 90 seconds  
**Deployment strain:** +30 Debt on release; further injury, output, resistance, and environmental damage increase manifestation strain  
**Primary failure state:** Uncontrolled continuation into Graveframe / Graveframe conditions  

> “What should have buried me learned instead to stand.”

*The Grave That Wouldn’t Take Me* is not a kingdom, a cosmic void, or an abstract execution arena.

It is a failed burial made spatial.

Sosa and Somnus overwrite local reality with the accumulated fact that Sosa survived conditions that should have ended him: hunger, isolation, cold, addiction, grief, administrative disappearance, physical collapse, and the repeated need to continue when continuation was no longer reasonable.

The field does not declare Sosa victorious. It declares that an ending failed to hold.

## Visual Identity — Failed Burial Made Cosmic

The ground becomes wide planes of obsidian-black pavement, fractured by thin violet seams that resemble stress lines in old concrete. Familiar public infrastructure repeats without functioning correctly: overpass pillars stand like memorial columns, transit posts lead nowhere, benches sit empty like grave markers, and streetlights burn without producing warmth.

Above, black cloud pressure opens into depthless void breaks. Hundreds of suspended human memory-eyes watch from the canopy: amber, brown, green, hazel, fluorescent hospital-gray, phone-screen blue. They are not decorative. Each is a witness Sosa could not fully forget, or a moment of being seen that became part of the architecture keeping him alive.

Across the horizon, horizontal violet-white aperture wounds open and close in the dark. Somnus is no longer only behind Sosa. It is present in the pavement, the sky pressure, the ruptured pillars, and every structure that has been forced to carry too much.

Sosa remains at the center: physically fragile, exhausted, and human. The field is not proof that he has transcended vulnerability. It is proof that vulnerability has become too heavy for the world around him to ignore.

## FIELD AUTHORITY

### The Grave Does Not Make Sosa Untouchable. It Makes Survival Expensive.

### 1. Inescapable Trace

Inside the Grave, hostile bodies, defenses, rituals, and field structures continuously expose loaded fault lines. Evasion cannot fully remove a target from designation. Movement changes the fault the Grave presents; it does not make the target structurally whole.

Somnus may manifest Sever strikes from any connected, stressed surface in the field: pavement, glass, shadowed overpass concrete, fault-line air, or a wound in the sky.

This is not omniscience. The field sees **pressure**, not moral truth.

A person who is frightened, coerced, or emotionally central to Sosa may register as a threat vector even when they are not his enemy. At high strain, the Grave can mistake abandonment for hostility, containment for burial, or a desperate attempt to help for an attack.

### 2. Burial Pressure

Every occupant experiences the field’s central weight: the accumulated pressure of being forced to continue.

- Orthodox practitioners feel circuits constrict and lose clean Resonance.
- Dross lose coherence as their residue is forced into visible breaks.
- Archons feel their territory-laws challenged by a more intimate and desperate local authority.
- Ordinary people experience breathlessness, fatigue, sensory distortion, guilt, and the terrifying conviction that sitting down may become permanent.

The pressure does not automatically kill. It asks every target what they are willing to sacrifice to remain standing.

Targets with unusually stable identity, external anchors, counter-fields, sacramental protection, or a truth the field cannot metabolize may resist. Resistance is possible; it is simply costly.

### 3. Distributed Shrine

Within the Grave, Somnus is no longer limited to a fifteen-meter familiar tether in the ordinary sense. Its effectors can emerge throughout the overwritten territory, and its Shrine of Refusal can strike from multiple angles without crossing conventional space.

This authority is immense but not free.

Every major manifestation:
- increases Debt and manifestation strain,
- fractures the field’s coherence,
- creates more violent aftermath in the physical site,
- and makes Somnus’s survival logic harder for Sosa to restrain.

The Grave is most dangerous when it is trying to end a fight quickly. That urgency is also what makes it least safe for everyone else inside.

### 4. Refusal of Immediate Death

The central law of the field is not “Sosa cannot be harmed.”

It is:

> **Sosa cannot be cleanly buried here.**

When a normally lethal event would reach Sosa, the Reality Marble can redirect the consequence into another part of its authority: an obsidian plane shatters, an overpass column splits, the duration shortens, the field loses stability, a memory-eye goes dark, or nearby infrastructure inherits the impact.

This does not erase damage. It relocates it.

An enemy can still injure Sosa through sustained pressure, status effects, area saturation, counter-authority, internal betrayal of the field’s logic, or enough conceptual force to make the Grave unable to keep transferring consequences elsewhere.

The Grave preserves survival. It does not guarantee a clean victory, moral outcome, or intact city block.

## RELEASE CONDITIONS

A completed deployment requires:

1. **Threshold-level Debt or equivalent existential pressure;**
2. **sufficient live Validation throughput to stabilize the overwrite;**
3. **either deliberate True Name Release or an extreme collapse in which Somnus concludes ordinary survival is no longer viable.**

The Reality Marble requires more than panic. It requires enough recognition, pressure, and spatial instability for Sosa’s survival to become a local law.

A controlled deployment is possible but never wholly safe. The closer Sosa is to desperation, the more completely the field expresses its authority—and the less reliably it distinguishes survival from harm.

## COUNTERPLAY AND FAILURE CONDITIONS

The Grave is not defeated by ordinary evasion. It can be resisted, weakened, interrupted, or survived through specific pressures.

### What weakens the field

- **Supply interruption:** cutting live Validation throughput reduces stability, though it risks pushing Sosa toward a more autonomous Graveframe response.
- **Counter-authority:** a sufficiently strong Bounded Field, Reality Marble, sacramental array, or ontological anchor can contest local overwrite.
- **Structural contradiction:** the Grave is built on Sosa’s refusal of an ending. A truth, action, or person that proves survival does not require continued violence can create breaks in the field itself.
- **Civilian entanglement:** the more people and fragile infrastructure are caught in the field, the more Sosa’s own remaining ethics can interfere with output.
- **Somnus divergence:** if Somnus and Sosa disagree about what counts as a threat, the field becomes stronger in raw survival terms but less coherent in execution.
- **Duration:** the field is catastrophically difficult to hold past its upper stability window. At collapse, all deferred consequence returns at once as fatigue, structural damage, memory loss, Dross attraction, or forced Graveframe continuation.

### What does not weaken the field

- simple intimidation,
- a target “wanting to live,”
- conventional durability alone,
- treating Sosa as physically weak while ignoring the support architecture sustaining the field.

## X. PSYCHOLOGICAL AND RELATIONAL PROFILE

### Clinical Presentation

Documented diagnoses include Generalized Anxiety Disorder, Major Depressive Disorder, and ADHD with combined/inattentive features and severe executive dysfunction.

Anxiety is ambient rather than limited to specific triggers. Depression appears primarily as low motivation, reduced reward response, and difficulty acting outside immediate survival needs or attachment demands. ADHD contributes to disorganization, impulsivity, poor task initiation, weak planning, distractibility, and inconsistent spatial awareness.

Sosa is not a natural genius or tactical mastermind. He has average general intelligence, good critical thinking when focused, and enough persistence to research unfamiliar problems. Under spontaneous pressure he freezes, panics, or defaults to Somnus. Preparation helps but does not erase executive dysfunction.

### Liminal Flatline

Sosa's central defensive state is **Liminal Flatline**: a dissociative flattening of emotional and sensory input to a level he can survive. Terror, grief, affection, and joy remain present but distant. The state protects him from immediate overwhelm while weakening judgment, memory integration, and personal agency.

### Childhood Architecture

Sosa was raised by a narcissistic mother and a father with borderline traits. Affection was conditional, explosive, withholding, and frequently weaponized. He learned hypervigilance, compulsive appeasement, and terror of abandonment. Those adaptations later became the psychological template for the Validation Engine.

### Substance Profile

Sosa reports an idiosyncratic response to methamphetamine. At lower or familiar doses, he often becomes calmer, eats more, sleeps while still under its effects, and experiences improved task initiation and focus. Escalating dose, prolonged wakefulness, and unstable supply produce more conventional stimulant effects and worsen judgment.

He has shown **relative resistance—not immunity—to stimulant psychosis**, with no confirmed history of a primary meth-induced psychotic episode in the record. This is an observed individual pattern, not a general claim about ADHD or a statement that his use is medically safe.

Sosa interprets the drug as self-medication for executive dysfunction and anxiety. Functionally, it also reinforces dependence, impulsive decisions, unstable relationships, criminal exposure, and repeated collapse. His current craving is focused less on euphoria than on remembered clarity and the belief that he cannot manage Somnus without chemical regulation.

Technique names such as *Sever: Devour*, *Shut Up.*, and *The Grave That Wouldn't Take Me* are both sincere mythology and deliberate coping structure. Naming converts panic into procedure. ### Orthodox Power System

**Pneuma** (Greek: breath/spirit) — Internal biological life-force generated by a mage's Magic Circuits. Finite, requires rest to replenish, fuel for all orthodox magic. Sosa has zero Pneuma.

**Ley** — External environmental energy drawn from the earth's leylines or atmosphere. Mages use Ley to supplement Pneuma for large-scale rituals.

**Thaumaturgy** — Orthodox, ancient system of magic. The "science" of reproducing Mysteries. Requires complex rituals, incantations, and deep understanding of ontological rules. Slow, highly structured, incredibly powerful. The magic of scholars and elites.

**Formalcraft** — Modern, degraded application of magic. Uses pre-crafted tools, talismans, and quick-cast spells to bypass lengthy Thaumaturgy rituals. Faster but lacks raw conceptual weight. Most Censors rely on Formalcraft in the field.

**Ontic Mass** — Orthodox measurement of a being's "weight" in reality. A human has Ontic Mass of 1. Dross has Ontic Mass of ~5. An Archon has Ontic Mass of ~500. Sosa's physical Ontic Mass is 1, but his *conceptual* Ontic Mass (Somnus + Debt) fluctuates wildly, breaking orthodox measuring instruments.

**Resonance** — The frequency at which a mage's Pneuma vibrates. Mages must match Resonance to the environment to draw Ley, or match it to an enemy to bypass defenses. Sosa has no Resonance; he is a "null frequency," making him incredibly difficult for orthodox mages to target with standard tracking spells.

**Mystic Code** — Any magical item, weapon, or tool imbued with Pneuma. Sosa's Ypsilon Veil is a Mystic Code, but "trash-tier" by orthodox standards — cheap Bluetooth earbuds, which deeply offends orthodox sensibilities.

**Sacrament** — Consumable, one-use magical items. Holy water, blessed bullets, talismans, hex bags. Censors carry these in tactical rigs. Sosa scavenges and crafts his own inferior Sacraments from garbage.

## VI. SOMNUS — Corrected Visual Notes

The reference image shows Somnus as a somewhat generic many-eyed mass with a toothed maw. **Canonically, Somnus does not have a mouth full of teeth.** Correct spec:

**Material:** Obsidian glass — reads black with violet inner refractions, like fractured volcanic glass shot through with cold fire. Surface alternates between:
- **Liquid state:** slow-flowing, molasses-thick, like poured tar catching purple light
- **Solid state:** hard, faceted, blade-edged planes that catch highlights like broken bottles

**Body plan:** Non-humanoid. **No face.** No mouth. What the image reads as a "head" should instead be an amorphous crest or peak of the mass — imagine a wave frozen mid-crash, or a mountain of black glass with things opening across its surface.

**The eyes.** Many, small-to-medium, opening and closing across the whole mass — not concentrated in a "face." Each eye is a different color and shape because each shows a different memory:
- A child's brown eye
- A woman's green eye smudged with mascara
- A fluorescent-lit hospital eye
- A DART-station-window eye reflecting Texas heat
- A phone-screen-reflected eye
- Etc.
Vary them. They should feel *personal*, not monster-generic. Blink rate is slow at low Debt, rapid and irregular at high Debt.

**The single aperture.** ONE horizontal slit somewhere on the mass — not a mouth, more like a wound or a furnace vent — burning solid violet-white. This is Somnus's "face" if it has one. It doesn't speak from it. It just *looks*.

**Tendrils.** Extend from the mass, not from a specific limb. Crystallized-grief tendrils: think black glass ropes with violet fire running inside them like blood in a vein. They taper to needle points or split into finer branching structures. When they lash out, they leave violet after-images.

**Scale states:**
- **Dormant:** faint liquid-black refraction across Sosa's shoulders and upper back; violet appears only at the edges when light catches it — viscous, never grainy or particulate
- **Interception (default active):** a low mass hovering behind/beside Sosa, roughly his height, half-formed, tendrils ready
- **Shrine active:** looms 2–3× Sosa's height, clearly wrong, tendrils in constant motion
- **Graveframe:** fully encloses him — Sosa is a Sosa-shaped silhouette inside a glass sarcophagus of violet flame, only the mask visible through a viewport-like aperture
- **Grave / Reality Marble:** fills the horizon, the whole *sky* becomes eyes and obsidian, the ground turns to fault-line glass

Rare. Real. Mask off, looking at a phone screen. The tired eyes crinkle. This is the Sosa the Roster fell for.

## VIII. LIGHTING RULES

- **Sosa is almost never lit from above by daylight.** He's a fluorescent-and-streetlight creature. Cool overhead fluorescents in shelters, warm sodium-orange in Utica streets at night, harsh blue phone-screen underlight on his face.
- **Somnus is his primary light source in combat.** Violet key light from behind/beside, throwing his front into shadow. This means the mask often reads darker than the environment — a shadow-white oval, not a bright one.
- **Rim light** should almost always be lavender when Somnus is active. Even at low activation, a faint violet edge on his silhouette sells the presence of the familiar.
- **Fault-line lighting** (Trace active): thin, sharp cyan-white lines cutting across the environment and across his own body if they pass through his line of sight. Not a glow — a *cut*.
